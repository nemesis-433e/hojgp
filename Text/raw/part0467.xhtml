【てちょうだい】

［V-てちょうだい］

（1）伸子のぶ こさん、ちょっとここへ来きてちょうだい。／Nobuko-san, come here for a minute, OK?
（2）彼女かの じょは「和雄かず おさん、ちょっと見みてちょうだい」と言いって、わたしを窓まどのところへ連つれて行いった。／She said, “Kazuo-san, please just take a look,” and led me over to the window.
（3）「お願ねがいだから、オートバイを乗のり回まわすのはやめてちょうだい」と母ははに言いわれた。／My mother told me, “I just want you to stop riding around on your motorcycle.”
Used to make a request of someone. Usually used by women or children to people with whom they have a close relationship. Although it is not a rough or impolite expression, it is not used in formal situations.
