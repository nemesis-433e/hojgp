【けれど】

1. けれど

（1）2時間じ かん待まった。けれど、彼かれは姿すがたを現あらわさなかった。／I waited for two hours. However, he did not show up.
（2）パーティーではだれも知しっている人ひとがいなかった。けれど、みんな親切しん せつでとても楽たのしかった。／There was no one I knew at the party. However, everybody was nice and I had a really good time.
（3）この作品さく ひんで3等賞とう しょうぐらいとれるかなと期待き たいしていた。けれど、結果けっ かは思おもいがけなく1等賞とう しょうだった。／I was hoping I might win third prize with this piece. But, to my surprise, the result was first prize.
Placed at the beginning of a sentence, けれど indicates that what comes next will develop in a way that differs from what might be expected from the preceding sentence(s). Compared with しかし, けれど is used more often in spoken language. However, it can be used in informal writing too.

2. ...けれど

（1）2時間じ かん待まったけれど、彼かれは姿すがたを現あらわさなかった。／I waited for two hours, but he did not show up.
（2）あの人ひとはきれいだけれど、意地悪い じ わるだ。／That woman is beautiful but ill-natured.
（3）下手へ ただけれど、ピアノを弾ひくのは楽たのしい。／I’m not good at it, but playing the piano is fun.
（4）野球や きゅうもおもしろいけれどサッカーはもっとおもしろいと思おもう若わかい人ひとが増ふえている。／More and more young people are starting to think that, although baseball is fun, soccer is even more fun.
（5）係長かかり ちょうはもうすぐ帰かえると思おもいますけれど、ここでお待まちになりますか。／I’m sure the subsection chief will be back soon...would you like to wait here?
Attaches to the end of a clause and signals that what comes next will develop in a way that is different from what might be anticipated from the statement in the preceding clause. It is an adversative expression, but not necessarily limited to adversative use. It can also be used to make a preliminary remark, as in (5). Tends to be used in spoken language, but may also be used in informal writing.

3. ...けれど

（1）いま母ははは留守る すなんですけれど。／My mother isn’t home right now....
（2）来週らい しゅうは外国がい こく出張しゅっ ちょうで、いないんですけれど。／I’m afraid I have an overseas business trip next week, so I won’t be here....
（3）紅茶こう ちゃは切きらしています。コーヒーならありますけれど。／We’ve run out of tea. We do have coffee, though....
（4）ちょっとコピー機きが動うごかないんですけれど。／Excuse me, but the copy machine isn’t working....
（5）書類しょ るいが一枚いち まい足たりないんですけれど。／A page of the document seems to be missing....
（6）かあさん、友達とも だちが夏休なつ やすみにうちへ泊とまりに来きたいって言いってるんだけれど。／Mom, a friend of mine says she wants to come and stay at our house during the summer break....
Placed at the end of a sentence, ...けれど creates the impression that the sentence is unfinished. Used to state an excuse, an explanation of circumstances, etc. in a soft and gentle manner. Also used to make indirect requests, as in (4), (5), and (6). Can be attached to both polite and plain forms. When attached to polite forms, it makes the expression more feminine and polite. Spoken language.
