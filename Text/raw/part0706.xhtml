【なら1】

When なら is attached directly to a noun, it expresses the topic of a sentence. The form ならば may also be used.

1. Nなら

（1）A:めがねはどこかな。／I wonder where my glasses are.
B:めがねならタンスの上うえに置おいてありましたよ。／Your glasses? They were on top of the dresser.
（2）A:アルバイトを雇やとうには金かねがかかりますよ。／Hiring a part-time worker will be expensive.
B:お金かねのことなら心配しん ぱいしなくていいですよ。何なんとかなりますから。／If it’s a matter of money, you don’t have to worry. We can manage.
（3）A:佐藤さ とうさん見みませんでしたか。／Have you seen Sato-san?
B:佐藤さ とうさんなら、図書館と しょ かんにいましたよ。／Sato-san? He was in the library.
（4）時間じ かんならば十分じゅう ぶんありますから、ご心配しん ぱいなく。／As for time, we have plenty of it, so don’t worry.
（5）例れいのことならもう社長しゃ ちょうに伝つたえてあります。／If you mean that thing we talked about, I’ve already informed the company president.
なら is used when the speaker takes something that the addressee has said, something mentioned during discussion of the previous topic, or something that can be predicted from the current situation, makes it a new topic, and then continues talking about it. Often used when the speaker adopts as a topic something that was previously brought up by the addressee.
なら can often be replaced by the topic marker は. However, なら essentially has the hypothetical meaning “if N were the topic,” while は does not have such meaning. Therefore, when なら is replaced by は, the meaning of the sentence changes slightly. なら is synonymous with Nだったら, and these two expressions are interchangeable.

2. NならNだ

（1）山やまならやっぱり富士山ふ じ さんだ。／When it comes to mountains, naturally it has to be Fuji-san (Mt. Fuji).
（2）ストレス解消法かい しょう ほうならゴルフに限かぎる。／If you’re looking for a way to get rid of stress, nothing beats golf.
（3）酒さけならなんといってもここの地酒じ ざけが一番いち ばんだ。／When it comes to sake, the locally brewed sake here is unquestionably the best.
（4）カキ料理りょう りなら広島ひろ しまが本場ほん ばだ。／If you want good oyster cuisine, Hiroshima is the place to go.
Nなら can be followed by Nだ, or by expressions such as Nに限る (there’s nothing better than N), Nが一番だ (N is the best), and Nがいい (N is good). Nなら is used to define the range of the topic and then describe the most highly evaluated item within that range. In this usage, Nなら can be replaced by Nは without any great change in meaning.

3. ...（助じょ詞し）なら

（1）あの人ひととなら結婚けっ こんしてもいい。／I wouldn’t mind getting married, if it were to him.
（2）フランス語ごはだめですが、英語えい ごでなら会話かい わができます。／I’m no good at French, but in English, I can carry on a conversation.
（3）あと一人ひとりだけなら入場にゅう じょうできます。／Only one more person can get in.
（4）A:足あしの具合ぐ あいはいかがですか。／How is your leg?
B:ゆっくりとなら歩あるけるようになりました。／If I go slowly, I can walk now.
（5）仕事し ごとの後あとなら時間じ かんがあります。／If it’s after work, I’ll have time.
When なら follows a noun, adverb, or noun/particle combination, it expresses the meaning that “Y doesn’t hold in other cases, but in the case of X, Y can be realized.” The Y clause that follows なら usually represents something desirable, and this pattern is used when the speaker actively selects an X that makes Y possible. This usage of なら is similar to the contrastive use of は. However, なら can be combined with interrogative words, while は cannot.
（○）何時なら都合がいいですか。／What time would be convenient for you?
（○）誰となら結婚してもいいですか。／Who would it have to be, in order for you to consider getting married?
（×）何時は都合がいいですか。／What is the time convenient for you?
（×）誰とは結婚してもいいですか。／I wouldn’t mind marrying who.
