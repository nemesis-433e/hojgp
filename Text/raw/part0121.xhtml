【がさいご】

［V-たがさいご］

（1）ここで会あったが最後さい ご、謝あやまってもらうまでは逃にがしはしない。／Now that I’ve met you here, I won’t let you go until you apologize to me.
（2）この計画けい かくを聞きいたが最後さい ご、あなたもグループに加くわわってもらおう。／Now that you’ve heard about this project, we’ll have you join the group.
（3）学校内がっ こう ないでタバコを吸すっているのを見みつかったが最後さい ご、停学てい がくは免まぬがれないだろう。／Now that they’ve been found smoking in school, they won’t be able to avoid suspension.
（4）その茶碗ちゃ わんは、一度いち ど手てに取とったが最後さい ご、どうしても買かわずにはいられなくなるほど手触て ざわりや重おもさ、色合いろ あいなどが私わたしの好このみに合あっていた。／Once I had picked up the bowl I couldn’t help buying it, since its feel, weight, and color were so much to my liking.
Expresses that a certain event occurs as the consequence of someone’s action or a course of events indicated in the subsequent clause. Example (1) expresses a challenge, meaning “I met you here, so you shall apologize to me.” Example (2) has the imperative meaning “because you have heard about this project, you have to join the group.” Also used in general, in non-threatening, non-imperative sentences such as (3) and (4).
