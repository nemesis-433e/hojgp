【だなんて】

（1）今頃いま ごろになって気きが変かわっただなんてよく言いえますね。／You have a lot of nerve, to say at this point that you’ve changed your mind.
（2）約束やく そくしたのに、できなかっただなんて、ひどい。／It’s outrageous that you couldn’t do it, even though you’d promised to.
（3）予約よ やくできるかもしれない、だなんて、無責任む せき にんな言いい方かたですね。／Saying that you might be able to make the reservation is an irresponsible way to put it.
（4）事故じ こで死しんでしまうだなんて、あんまりだ。／It’s really too much that they were killed in an accident.
Used to criticize or complain by repeating what the other person has said. In some cases expresses a feeling of criticism or grief with respect to a circumstance that is not the speaker’s fault, as in (4). なんて is also used by itself.
