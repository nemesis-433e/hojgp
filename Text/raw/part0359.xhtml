【それを】

（1）あれほど考かんがえ直なおすように言いったのに君きみは会社かい しゃをやめた。それを今いまになってもう一度いち ど雇やとってくれだなんて、いったい何なにを考かんがえてるんだ。／I told you so many times to reconsider, but you still quit the company. And now you’re asking me to rehire you—what in the world are you thinking?
（2）A:もう一度いち どやり直なおそうよ。／Let’s give it another try.
B:別わかれようって言いったのはあなたよ。それを今いまさらなによ。／You’re the one who said you wanted to break up. And now you’re saying this?
（3）契約けい やくしたのは1年前ねん まえだ。それを今いまになって解約かい やくしたいと言いってきても無理む りだ。／It was a year ago that we fixed the contract. You can’t all of a sudden say you want to cancel it now.
This expression means “despite that; and yet.” It is used to criticize another person about a current situation that involves some kind of change from a previous situation. It is often used with 今さら (now; at this late date) and 今になって (now that things have come to such a pass).
