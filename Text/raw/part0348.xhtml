【それとも】

1. NそれともN

（1）A:コーヒー？　それとも紅茶こう ちゃ？／Coffee? Or tea?
B:どちらでもけっこうです。／Either one is fine.
（2）A:あしたのパーティーには、何なにを着きて行いくつもり？　着物き もの、それともドレス？／What are you going to wear to the party tomorrow? A kimono, or a dress?
B:まだ、決きめてないのよ。／I haven’t decided yet.
（3）進学しん がくか、それとも就職しゅう しょくかとずいぶん悩なやんだ。／Going to graduate school, or finding a job—I worried a lot about which to do.
Takes the pattern XそれともY or XかそれともYか and means “either X or Y.” Used to indicate two possibilities and ask the listener which is better, as in (1), or to ask about the listener’s intention, as in (2). Cannot be used when giving instructions, as seen in the examples below.
（×）黒それとも青のインクで書いてください。／Please use a pen with black, or blue ink.
（○）黒か青のインクで書いてください。／Please use a pen with black or blue ink.
Also used when there are two possibilities and the speaker is unsure or doesn’t know which one to choose, as in (3). In this case, それとも can be replaced by あるいは (or).

2. ...それとも

（1）雨あめが降ふってきましたが、どうしますか。行いきますか。それとも延期えん きしますか。／It’s started raining, so what shall we do? Shall we go? Or shall we postpone it?
（2）洋室よう しつがよろしいですか、それとも和室わ しつの方ほうがよろしいですか。／Would you prefer a Western-style room, or would a Japanese-style room be better?
（3）A:散歩さん ぽにでも行いく？　それとも、映画えい がでも見みようか。／Do you want to take a walk? Or would you like to see a film?
B:そうね、久ひさしぶりに映画えい がもいいな。／Hmm... a movie might be nice, since we haven’t seen one in a while.
（4）就職しゅう しょくしようか、それとも進学しん がくしようかと迷まよっている。／I can’t decide whether to find a job or to go on to university.
（5）彼かれは、初はじめから来くるつもりがなかったのか、それとも、急きゅうに気きが変かわったのか、約束やく そくの時間じ かんが過すぎても現あらわれなかった。／I don’t know if he didn’t intend to come in the first place, or if he all of a sudden changed his mind, but he never showed up, even after the appointed time.
（6）この手紙て がみを読よんで、彼女かの じょは喜よろこんでくれるだろうか。それとも、軽蔑けい べつするだろうか。／I wonder if she’ll be happy when she reads this letter. Or if she’ll despise me.
Takes the pattern XそれともY or XかそれともYか and means “either X or Y.” (1)-(3) are examples of the speaker indicating two possibilities and asking the listener which is better. (4)-(6) are examples in which there are two possibilities, and the speaker is either unsure which is true or doesn’t know which to choose. In (4)-(6), それとも can be replaced by あるいは (or).
