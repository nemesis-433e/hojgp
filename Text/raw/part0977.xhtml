【もう2】

1. もう〈completion〉

（1）今日きょうの仕事し ごとはもう全部ぜん ぶ終おわった。／Today’s work is all done.
（2）A:今いま評判ひょう ばんになっているあの映画えい が、もう見みましたか。／Have you seen that film that everyone’s talking about now?
B:ええ、この前まえの日にち曜日よう びに見みました。／Yes, I watched it last Sunday.
（3）食事しょく じはもうできている。／Dinner is ready.
（4）その問題もん だいなら、もう解決かい けつしている。／That problem? It’s been resolved already.
（5）彼かれの娘むすめはもう大学だい がくを卒業そつ ぎょうしたそうだ。／I heard that his daughter has already graduated from college.
（6）駅えきについたときにもう特急とっ きゅうは出でてしまっていた。／When I got to the station, the limited express had already left.
（7）手紙て がみはもう投函とう かんしたので、取とり返かえせないんです。／I have already mailed the letter, so I can’t get it back.
（8）A:すみません、今日きょうはもう閉店へい てんですか。／Excuse me, are you already closed for today?
B:いいえ、まだ開あいています。／No, we’re still open.
（9）A:この本ほんはもう出でましたか。／Is this book out yet?
B:いいえ、まだ出でていません。予定よ ていは来週らい しゅうです。／No, not yet. It is scheduled (to be out) next week.
When used with a verb phrase, this indicates the completion of a deed or event by a certain point in time. It is also used in interrogative sentences asking whether or not something has been completed. When the point of completion has not yet been reached, まだ...ない is used in both declarative and interrogative sentences.

2. もう　+時じ間かん／+年ねん齢れい

（1）おしゃべりに夢中む ちゅうになっていたら、もう5時じだ。／We got lost in conversation and it’s already five o’clock.
（2）気きがついたらもう朝あさだった。／Before we knew it, it was already morning.
（3）この子こはもう 10 才さいだから、十分じゅう ぶん事故じ この証人しょう にんになれる。／This child is already ten years old, so she can serve as a witness to an accident.
（4）こよみの上うえではもう春はるなのに、まだ雪ゆきが降ふっている。／Although the calendar says it is spring, it is still snowing.
（5）もう夜よが明あけるのに彼かれらはまだ話はなし続つづけている。／They kept on talking even though dawn was breaking.
（6）もう8時じですよ。起おきなさい、学校がっ こうに遅おくれますよ。／It’s already eight o’clock. Get up, or you’ll be late for school.
Used with expressions which show time or age, it shows that a point which is rather late has been reached. As in examples (1) and (2), it includes the feeling that a certain point in time has been reached sooner than was thought.

3. もうNa だ／もういい

（1）もうおなかが一杯いっ ぱいだ。／I’m already full.
（2）今日きょうはもう十分じゅう ぶんに楽たのしんだ。／I’ve already had a lot of fun today.
（3）A:お湯ゆはわいていますか。／Is the water boiling yet?
B:ええ、もういいですよ。／Yes, it’s ready.
（4）A:機械き かい、直なおったんですか。／Is the machine fixed?
B:ええ、これでもういいはずです。／Yes, it should be fine now.
（5）A:ちょっと目めを閉とじて。1、2、3。／Just shut your eyes. One, two, three.
B:もういい？／Okay now?
A:いいよ。はい、目めを開あけて。／Okay, open your eyes.
Used with phrases such as 一杯だ and 十分だ, this means “(it) ought to be enough/satisfactory.” もういい, basically meaning “a satisfactory situation or state has been reached,” is used in various circumstances. Depending on the context, it can mean “to have done enough,” or “to have resolved (it).” For the negative usage rules for もういい, please see Usage 5b of もう.

4. もう...ない

（1）山田やま ださんはもうここにはいません。／Yamada-san is no longer here.
（2）この喫茶店きっ さ てんはもう営業えい ぎょうしていない。／This coffee shop is no longer in business.
（3）疲つかれて、もう何なにも考かんがえられなくなった。／I was so tired I couldn’t think anymore.
（4）交渉こう しょうのあと、だれももう文句もん くを言いわなかった。／After the negotiations, nobody had any more complaints.
（5）かれとは、もうこれ以上い じょう話はなしたくない。／I don’t want to say anything more to him.
（6）わたしは、18 才さい、もう子供こ どもではない。／I’m eighteen. I’m not a child anymore.
（7）もう二度に どとあの人ひとには会あわないだろう。／I don’t suppose I’ll ever meet her again.
（8）もう誰だれも信しんじられないと言いって、彼女かの じょは泣ないていた。／She was crying and said, “I can’t trust anyone anymore.”
（9）こんな待遇たい ぐうの悪わるい職場しょく ばにはもうがまんができない。／I can’t take it anymore in a workplace like this with such terrible conditions.
（10）さいふの中なかにはもう 100 円えんしか残のこっていなかったので、家いえへ帰かえるのにバスにも乗のれなかった。／Since I only had 100 yen left in my wallet, I couldn’t even catch the bus home.
（11）10 万まん円えんの値段ね だんがついたので、もうこれ以上い じょうは上あがらないだろうと思おもった。／Because it was already priced at 100,000 yen, I didn’t think the price could go any higher.
This means, with something like a certain point in time as the limit, there is nothing further or nothing after that.

5. もう〈negative attitude〉

a. もう+否ひ定てい的てき表ひょう現げん

（1）こんな退屈たい くつな仕事し ごとはもうやめたい。／I want to quit this kind of boring work.
（2）もうあの人ひとの愚痴ぐ ちを聞きくのはいやだ。／I’m sick of hearing that person’s complaints.
（3）これ以上い じょう歩あるき続つづけるのは体力的たい りょく てきにもう無理む りです。／It’s physically impossible for me to walk any further.
（4）あの人ひとをかばい続つづけられるのももう限界げん かいだ。／I can’t carry on sticking up for that man.
（5）戦争せん そうをするのは、もうたくさんだ。／I’ve had it with the war.
（6）こんなまずいものを食たべるのはもうたくさんだ。／I can’t eat any more of such disgusting stuff.
（7）もういいかげんに妹いもうとをいじめるのはやめなさい。／Stop picking on your younger sister.
Using predicates with a negative meaning such as 無理だ and いやだ, this means the situation cannot be tolerated any further. Meaning “the limit has been reached and so I can’t take anymore,” もうたくさんだ is often used when the speaker is feeling quite emotional. It can also be used in cases, such as example (7), which prohibit further such behavior.

b. もういい

（1）A:ほかに出だす書類しょ るいがありますか。／Do you have any other documents to hand in?
B:これでもういいです。／That’s all.
（2）A:チョコレート買かいましょうか。／Shall we buy some chocolate?
B:いや、これだけ食料しょく りょうがあれば、もういいです。／No, this should be enough food.
（3）A:もう一杯いっ ぱいいかがですか。／How about one more (glass/cup)?
B:いや、もういいです。／No, I’ve had enough.
（4）A:急きゅうなアルバイトさえなかったら、来こられたんだけど。／If I just hadn’t had that sudden part-time work, I could have come.
B:言いい訳わけはもういいよ。／That’s enough of your excuses.
（5）A:お母かあさんの気持き もちも考かんがえてみなさい。／Try to think about your mother’s feelings, too.
B:もういいよ。お説教せっ きょうは聞きき飽あきたよ。／Enough! I’m tired of your preaching!
（6）A:頑張がん ばっていたのに、うまく行いかなくて残念ざん ねんだったね。／It’s such a shame that it didn’t go well even though you did your best.
B:もういいんです。何なにか、ほかの事ことを考かんがえます。／Oh, it’s okay. I will think of something else.
（7）A:もう一回いっ かい探さがしなおせば、みつかるかもしれません。／If we have another look, we may be able to find it.
B:もういいよ。あきらめよう。／I’ve had enough! Let’s give it up.
Fundamentally this means “because this is enough, more than this is not necessary.” It is used in various circumstances. In example (3) it is used to decline something. Also, in (4) and (5), meaning “this is the limit, I can’t accept more than this,” it conveys the speaker’s attitude of refusal. It is often used in cases in which something has become disagreeable or tedious. As in (6) and (7), it is also used as an expression which conveys the feeling of being sick of or giving up on something which had been adhered to until that point. For the affirmative usage rules of もういい, see Usage 3 of もう.

6. もう〈criticism〉

（1）お母かあさんたら、もう。わたしの友達とも だちの悪口わる ぐちを言いうのはやめてよ。／Mom, enough! Stop badmouthing my friends.
（2）もう、あなたったら、こんなやさしい計算けい さんもできないの。／Oh, come on, dear. Can’t you even do a simple calculation like this?
（3）山田やま ださんたら、もう、また「お茶入ちゃ いれて」ですって。自分じ ぶんでやればいいのに。／That Yamada-san telling me again, “Make some tea.” He should make it himself.
（4）A:あ、また、汚よごした。／Oh, it’s dirty again.
B:もう。／Already again!
At the start or in the middle of a sentence, this expresses a feeling of criticism towards the other person. Only used in casual conversation and is often used by women. Frequently used with the highly critical (っ)たら.
