【かな】

（1）山田やま ださんは今日きょう来くるかな。／I wonder if Yamada-san will come today.
（2）これ、おいしいのかな。／I wonder if this is tasty.
（3）これ、もらって帰かえってもいいのかな。／I wonder if I can take this home.
（4）ちょっと手伝て つだってくれないかな。／I wonder if you would give me a hand?
（5）今度こん どの旅行りょ こうはどこへ行いこうかな。／Where should I go on my next trip?
（6）最近さい きんなんでこんなに疲つかれやすいのかなあ。／I wonder why I get tired so easily these days.
かな is formed by attaching な to か, which is a marker indicating a question. かなis used at the end of a sentence and conveys the feeling of asking oneself a question. Although it expresses self-directed wondering or pondering, when uttered to another person it can be interpreted as an indirect request for assistance, permission, etc. Not combined with polite forms. A casual expression used in spoken language. The final sound な may be lengthened so it is pronounced as ...かなあ.
